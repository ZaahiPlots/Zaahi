## Orientation

This document specifies three founder-specific protections for Dymo Tsvyk as Co-founder and Operations Principal of ZAAHI. The structure mirrors Zhan's protection package (`ZHAN_PROTECTIONS.md`); both founders receive symmetric, milestone-aligned, operating-role-linked protections. Zhan's performance bonus is anchored to the Platform (which he leads as CEO/CTO); Dymo's is anchored to the Agency (which he leads as Operations Principal).

The three protections are:

1. **Performance Bonus Shares (Agency)** — additional equity of up to 15 % cumulative in the Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC), triggered by milestone achievement.
2. **Co-founder Salary Floor** — guaranteed minimum monthly compensation from the Agency operating account while Dymo actively serves as Operations Principal.
3. **Tag-Along Rights and Right of First Refusal** — on any share transfer by Rudi.

These protections are independent of and additional to:

- Dymo's base equity (45 % of Agency; 10 % of Platform) as set out in `MOU_RUDI.md` §2 and `TERM_SHEET.md` §6.
- Dymo's full anti-dilution protection to IPO in both entities (per `MOU_RUDI.md` §3 and `TERM_SHEET.md` §8).
- Dymo's 4-year reverse vesting with 1-year cliff (per `MOU_RUDI.md` §7 and `TERM_SHEET.md` §19).
- Dymo's non-compete and non-solicit commitments (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §22).

These protections are codified in a dedicated schedule to the Shareholders Agreement, approved by all three Shareholders (Zhan, Dymo, Rudi). None of them reduce Rudi's economic entitlements or governance rights; they are funded from the Agency's founder equity pool (Zhan's Agency 45 % dilutes first, with his 10 % Agency floor preserved) or from Agency treasury reserve, not from Rudi's 10 %.

## 1. Performance Bonus Shares (Agency)

### 1.1 Summary

Dymo is entitled to receive additional equity in the Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC) — called **Performance Bonus Shares** — upon achievement of specified performance milestones. Each milestone triggers **+5.0 % additional equity**, up to a **cumulative maximum of 15.0 %**.

### 1.2 Triggers

Any one of the following, each independently, triggers a single 5 % bonus issuance:

**Trigger A — Agency cumulative 100 deals closed.**
Measured as the cumulative count of agency deals where DLD transfer certificates have been issued and commissions recorded by the Agency. Each deal counts once regardless of size. Validation by Board minutes and Agency accounting records. (Note: this is the same metric as Zhan's Trigger B; a single deal-100 milestone can therefore fire both founders' bonuses simultaneously, Dymo at Agency level and Zhan at Platform level — by design, reflecting that the milestone is jointly achieved.)

**Trigger B — Agency annual revenue reaches AED 10,000,000.**
Measured as Agency gross commission revenue over any trailing 12-month period. Includes all commission and brokerage fee revenue earned by the Agency entity. Excludes Platform revenue (which is separately tracked in the Platform entity). Validation by the Agency's annual audit or by a review performed by a Big-4 accounting firm at the Company's election.

**Trigger C — Successful completion of a Series A equity financing of not less than USD 2,000,000.**
Measured as: (a) execution of a priced equity financing agreement, (b) receipt of funds, and (c) closing of the round with at least one institutional lead investor. SAFEs, convertible notes, or friends-and-family rounds do not count; a formal priced round with institutional participation is required. Validation by the Board resolution and receipt of funds into the Platform or Agency bank account. (Note: this is the same metric as Zhan's Trigger C; a Series A close fires both founders' bonuses simultaneously, by design.)

### 1.3 Maximum cap

Total cumulative Performance Bonus Shares issued to Dymo shall not exceed **15.0 % of the fully-diluted equity of the Agency** (Dubai Mainland LLC), representing three completed triggers. Once the 15 % cap is reached, no further Performance Bonus Shares are issued regardless of additional milestone achievements.

### 1.4 Vesting and issuance timing

Performance Bonus Shares are:

- **Issued within 30 days** of the Board's verification of the milestone achievement.
- **Fully vested immediately upon issuance** (no additional vesting period, unlike Dymo's base equity).
- Subject to the same transfer restrictions, ROFR, and tag-along provisions as Dymo's base equity.

### 1.5 Source of shares

Performance Bonus Shares are issued as newly-issued shares of the Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC). To preserve Rudi's full anti-dilution protection and Zhan's 10 % floor, the issuance is structured such that:

- Rudi's 10.0 % of the Agency is preserved (full-ratchet anti-dilution to IPO).
- Zhan's equity in the Agency dilutes in the ordinary course but never below the contractual 10 % floor.
- The bonus equity is effectively drawn from Zhan's Agency base equity pool (diluting Zhan's Agency share pro-rata down toward — but never below — the 10 % floor) or from the Agency treasury reserve once the floor binds.

**Mechanism.** At issuance, Dymo's fully-diluted ownership of the Agency moves from his then-current percentage to his then-current percentage plus 5.0 %. Rudi's 10 % is preserved through anti-dilution issuance. Zhan absorbs the dilution pro-rata from his Agency share, subject to his 10 % Agency floor. If Zhan's Agency share is already at the 10 % floor when a Dymo bonus trigger fires, the incremental 5 % is sourced from Agency treasury reserve.

### 1.6 Example scenarios

**Scenario A — Agency closes 100 deals cumulative in Month 36.**
Trigger A fires. Dymo receives 5 % additional Agency equity. Agency cap table moves from (Rudi 10 % / Dymo 45 % / Zhan 45 %) to approximately (Rudi 10 % / Dymo 50 % / Zhan 40 %). Cumulative Dymo bonus issued: 5 %. Maximum remaining: 10 %. Same event also fires Zhan's Trigger B at Platform level (5 % Platform bonus to Zhan).

**Scenario B — Agency annual revenue hits AED 10 M in Month 30 AND a USD 2 M Series A closes in Month 36.**
Triggers B and C fire (separate events). Dymo receives 10 % additional Agency equity (5 % per trigger). Cumulative Dymo bonus issued: 10 %. Maximum remaining: 5 %.

**Scenario C — All three triggers fire over Years 2–5.**
Dymo receives the full 15 % cumulative Agency bonus. Maximum reached. No further bonuses. Dymo's Agency equity caps at approximately 60 % (45 % base + 15 % bonus, before any Series dilution).

**Scenario D — Trigger B fires and Agency revenue keeps growing.**
Trigger B fires once at AED 10 M. Subsequent revenue growth (AED 20 M, 30 M, etc.) does not re-fire the trigger. The next bonus requires a different Trigger (A or C).

**Scenario E — Agency closes 100 deals AND hits AED 10 M revenue in the same quarter.**
Two separate triggers (A + B). Dymo receives 10 % in that quarter (5 % per trigger). Board confirms both milestones met in the same Board resolution.

### 1.7 Founder departure

If Dymo voluntarily departs or is terminated for Cause (as defined in the Shareholders Agreement) before a trigger fires, the opportunity for that particular trigger lapses. If Dymo is terminated without Cause, triggers achieved within the following 12 months are still honoured (same terms, same issuance mechanism). This mirrors Zhan's equivalent protection in `ZHAN_PROTECTIONS.md` §1.7.

## 2. Co-founder Salary Floor

### 2.1 Summary

While Dymo is actively engaged as Operations Principal of the Agency, the Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC) shall pay Dymo a **monthly salary of not less than AED 30,000–50,000**, calibrated at SHA execution based on the Agency's cash position and the prevailing UAE market rate for Operations Principal / General Manager compensation at a pre-Series-A real estate brokerage.

### 2.2 Calibration

The specific Co-founder Salary figure within the AED 30,000–50,000 range is calibrated at SHA execution and reviewed annually at the Annual General Meeting. Suggested calibration framework:

| Year | Suggested base salary (AED / month) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 (May 2026 – Apr 2027) | 30,000 – 35,000 | Company in launch phase; first deals establishing revenue flow |
| Year 2 (May 2027 – Apr 2028) | 35,000 – 42,000 | Agency revenue validated; second agent hired; pre-Series A |
| Year 3 (May 2028 – Apr 2029) | 42,000 – 50,000 | Post-Series A (if raised); multi-agent operation compounding |
| Year 4+ | Market-benchmarked | Post-maturity; benchmark to UAE brokerage Operations Principal market |

These are illustrative. The final calibration is agreed at SHA execution.

### 2.3 Payable source

- Salary is paid **monthly from the Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC) operating account; pro-rated for partial months**.
- Funded from Agency gross commission revenue before any quarterly dividend declaration.
- Tax and social security: treated as employment income under UAE Federal Decree-Law 33/2021; end-of-service gratuity accrued per UAE Labour Law.

### 2.4 Conditions

- Payable while Dymo actively serves as Operations Principal of the Agency (inclusive of role evolution — Head of Sales, General Manager, or similar operational title — as the Agency matures).
- Suspended if Dymo voluntarily takes extended leave of absence exceeding 30 consecutive days (except for medically-justified leave or Board-approved sabbatical).
- **Terminated** if Dymo voluntarily resigns.
- **Terminated** if Dymo is terminated for Cause (as defined in the Shareholders Agreement).
- **6 months' severance** paid as a lump sum or monthly continuation (at Dymo's election) if Dymo is terminated without Cause.

### 2.5 Priority

The Co-founder Salary is a priority operating expense of the Agency. It accrues monthly and must be paid before any quarterly dividend declaration under the Dividend Policy (`PROFIT_DISTRIBUTION_MECHANICS.md` §2.1). If in any quarter the Agency's cash position cannot support both the salary and a dividend, the salary is paid first and the dividend is deferred or reduced.

### 2.6 Relationship with Zhan compensation

Zhan receives his own salary as CEO/CTO of the Platform, payable monthly from the Platform operating account (per `ZHAN_PROTECTIONS.md` §2). Dymo's Co-founder Salary Floor is specific to Dymo and is a parallel, symmetric obligation at the Agency entity. The two salary obligations are independent and do not offset each other; both founders draw salaries from their respective entities for the work they perform at those entities.

## 3. Tag-Along Rights and Right of First Refusal on Rudi transfers

### 3.1 Summary

If Rudi proposes to transfer any of his shares (in either the Agency or the Platform) to a third party, Dymo is entitled to:

- **Tag-Along Right** — sell a proportional amount of his own shares in the same entity on the same terms; and
- **Right of First Refusal (ROFR)** — match the third-party offer and acquire Rudi's shares instead.

These rights mirror Zhan's equivalent rights in `ZHAN_PROTECTIONS.md` §3, coordinate with any Company-level ROFR (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §16), and apply in parallel with any Zhan-level matching rights.

### 3.2 Tag-Along Right

Tag-Along is triggered whenever Rudi delivers notice of a proposed transfer of any of his shares after the second anniversary of the Closing Date (the earliest date Rudi may transfer per `TERM_SHEET.md` §16(a)).

- **Notice period:** minimum 30 days from Rudi's transfer notice to Dymo's election deadline.
- **Exercise window:** 30 days from receipt of Rudi's notice.

If exercised:

- Dymo may sell up to a proportional amount of his own shares in the same entity (Agency or Platform) on the same per-share terms as Rudi's proposed sale.
- The proportion is Dymo's-holding / (Rudi-holding + Dymo-holding) applied to the total number of shares being sold.
- The third-party purchaser is obligated to purchase the additional shares on the same economic terms or the transfer cannot proceed.

**Purpose.** Tag-Along prevents Rudi from exiting on terms that Dymo cannot match, leaving Dymo trapped with an unknown new shareholder.

### 3.3 Right of First Refusal

Independently of Tag-Along, Dymo has a ROFR on Rudi's proposed transfer. On receipt of Rudi's transfer notice, Dymo has **30 days** to elect to purchase Rudi's shares on the same economic terms as the third-party offer.

- If Dymo exercises ROFR, he purchases Rudi's shares at the third-party price.
- Dymo's ROFR is subordinate to the Company's ROFR (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §16(a)) — i.e., if the Company elects to purchase, Dymo's ROFR lapses.
- Dymo's ROFR is in parallel with Zhan's ROFR (per `ZHAN_PROTECTIONS.md` §3.3); if both Zhan and Dymo elect to exercise, the shares are divided pro-rata to their respective shareholdings.

**Purpose.** ROFR gives Dymo direct control over who becomes a shareholder. It is stronger than Tag-Along because Dymo can unilaterally consolidate rather than merely participate.

### 3.4 Sequencing

The sequencing of rights on a Rudi transfer notice is:

1. **Company ROFR** — 30 days. If exercised, the Company purchases Rudi's shares (treasury shares).
2. **Zhan ROFR + Dymo ROFR** (pro-rata if both elect) — a further 30 days if the Company declines.
3. **Zhan Tag-Along + Dymo Tag-Along** (pro-rata if both elect) — a further 30 days if neither ROFR is exercised.
4. **Third-party transfer** — the remaining shares are transferred to the third party on the original proposed terms.

Total maximum timeline from Rudi's notice to final transfer: 90 days.

### 3.5 Permitted transfers

The Tag-Along and ROFR do not apply to Rudi's permitted transfers (as defined in `TERM_SHEET.md` §16(b)): transfers to wholly-owned entities of Rudi, to a family trust or estate structure, or with the prior written consent of the other Shareholders.

### 3.6 Example

Rudi proposes to sell his 10 % of the Platform to a third-party fund for AED 3,000,000.

1. The Company has 30 days to match at AED 3,000,000. It declines.
2. Zhan and Dymo each have 30 days to exercise ROFR. At Platform level, Zhan holds 80 % and Dymo holds 10 %. If both exercise, they purchase Rudi's 10 % pro-rata to their respective holdings: Zhan buys 80 / (80 + 10) = 88.9 % of Rudi's shares (~ 8.9 %) at AED 2.67 M; Dymo buys 10 / 90 = 11.1 % of Rudi's shares (~ 1.1 %) at AED 333 k. If only Zhan exercises, he purchases the full 10 % at AED 3,000,000.
3. If both decline ROFR, they can exercise Tag-Along: each may sell a proportional amount of their own shares to the third party.
4. If both decline all options, the third-party purchase of Rudi's 10 % proceeds.
