## Selected mechanism

The Parties have selected **Option B — Dividend Policy codified in the Shareholders Agreement** over alternative mechanics such as inter-company service agreements or direct capital contributions. The reasons:

- **Simplicity and auditability.** A standard dividend declaration is legible to any accountant, tax authority, and future investor.
- **Compatible with preferred-share structure.** Rudi's 10 % is Preferred Shares in the Agency post-SAFE conversion; preferred dividends are the natural entitlement mechanism.
- **UAE tax-predictable.** Corporate Tax treatment of dividends is well-established under UAE Federal Decree-Law 47/2022; inter-company flows benefit from the Qualifying Free Zone Person / Small Business Relief regimes.
- **Board-controlled.** Quarterly Board resolutions give Zhan + Dymo + Rudi visibility into each declaration and prevent ad-hoc distributions.
- **Contractually firm.** Codifying the 10 / 10 / 10 / 70 split as a binding SHA clause (not a discretionary dividend policy) removes the ambiguity that could otherwise arise around "when" and "how much" is distributed.

## 1. Legal framework

### 1.1 The contractual basis

The Dividend Policy is set out in Schedule [X] of the Shareholders Agreement ("**SHA**"). The SHA binds the Company (Agency entity, Dubai Mainland LLC) and each Shareholder (Zhan, Dymo, Rudi). The Dividend Policy operates alongside UAE Federal Decree-Law 32/2021 (Commercial Companies Law) and the Articles of Association of the Agency LLC.

### 1.2 Enforceability

Under UAE law, dividend distributions must be:
- Declared from distributable reserves (i.e., from accumulated profit, after all operating expenses, taxes, statutory reserves, and any contractual reserves).
- Approved by the Board (and where applicable, ratified by the General Assembly of shareholders).
- Compliant with the minimum-capital and solvency tests under UAE Companies Law.

The Dividend Policy clause in the SHA contractually commits the Company and the Board to declare dividends according to the fixed 10 / 10 / 10 / 70 ratio, subject only to the operating reserve threshold set out below. Failure by the Board to declare within the agreed timeframe is a breach of the SHA, enforceable by any Shareholder.

### 1.3 Governing law and disputes

The Dividend Policy is governed by Dubai law (as the Agency is a Dubai Mainland LLC). Disputes arising from its interpretation or application are resolved through the dispute-resolution mechanism in the SHA (ADGM arbitration, English language, Abu Dhabi seat) — except for any dispute that under UAE mandatory procedural law must be heard before the Dubai Courts.

## 2. Calculation methodology

### 2.1 Definition of Distributable Net Profit

For each calendar quarter, the Distributable Net Profit is calculated as:

**Distributable Net Profit = Gross Commission Revenue − Direct Costs − Allocated OpEx − Corporate Tax − VAT Reconciliation − Statutory Reserve − Contractual Operating Reserve**

where:

- **Gross Commission Revenue** = sum of all agency commissions invoiced and received (or recognised under the Company's accrual accounting policy) during the quarter.
- **Direct Costs** = deal-level costs directly attributable to closed transactions: Trakheesi permits, client-entertainment, external due-diligence fees, translation fees, notary fees, courier and document-production fees.
- **Allocated OpEx** = pro-rata share of quarterly operating expenses: founder salaries, agent salaries, office lease, utilities, software subscriptions, accounting, legal retainer, marketing, insurance, all other general administrative expenses.
- **Corporate Tax** = 9 % UAE CT on taxable income above the AED 375,000 threshold, provisioned on a quarterly basis. If the Company qualifies for Small Business Relief (revenue ≤ AED 3 M annualised), CT provision is zero.
- **VAT Reconciliation** = VAT collected from clients minus VAT paid to suppliers, settled with FTA. VAT is pass-through and does not reduce distributable profit, but must be properly ring-fenced in the calculation.
- **Statutory Reserve** = 10 % of annual net profit, credited to a non-distributable legal reserve until the reserve equals 50 % of the issued share capital (per UAE Commercial Companies Law).
- **Contractual Operating Reserve** = a rolling cash balance equivalent to at least three (3) months of forecast OpEx, to ensure the Company maintains working-capital solvency. The target reserve is AED 450,000 minimum at all times (based on Year 1 OpEx of ~AED 145,000 per month).

### 2.2 Distribution formula

Once Distributable Net Profit is determined for the quarter, it is split as follows:

| Beneficiary | Share of Distributable Net Profit | Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Rudi** | 10.0 % | Preferred Share dividend |
| **Dymo** | 10.0 % | Common Share dividend |
| **Zhan** | 10.0 % | Common Share dividend |
| **Platform Development Fund (ADGM HoldCo)** | 70.0 % | Inter-company transfer (see §3 below) |

## 3. Inter-company flow (70 % to Platform)

The 70 % share destined for the Platform Development Fund is not a dividend (it is not going to a shareholder of the Agency). It is a transfer from the Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC) to the Platform (ADGM HoldCo). The legal mechanism for this flow is one of the following, to be finalised by UAE tax counsel:

### Option 3.1 — Inter-company Service Agreement (preferred)

The Agency pays the Platform a **service fee** for the licensed use of the ZAAHI platform technology, brand, 3D Signature IP, Ambassador program, and ongoing technical support. The fee is structured as "70 % of Distributable Net Profit, payable quarterly within 15 days of Dividend Declaration Date."

**Advantages:**
- Clean accounting (fee is a deductible expense of Agency, taxable income of Platform).
- No shareholder-level tax implications.
- Aligns with transfer-pricing arm's-length principle if documented properly.

**Requirements:**
- Transfer-pricing documentation (required anyway under UAE CT Federal Decree-Law 47/2022 for related-party transactions).
- Service Agreement signed between Agency and Platform.
- Quarterly invoice from Platform to Agency for the calculated fee.

### Option 3.2 — Shareholder Loan / Capital Contribution

The Agency makes a **capital contribution** or **shareholder loan** to the Platform (which is the 100 % parent or sister company, depending on structure). This operates as balance-sheet transfer, not P&L expense.

**Disadvantages:**
- Does not reduce Agency taxable income (harder CT position).
- Capital contribution creates complex cap-table accounting between entities.

**Recommendation:** the Service Agreement approach (Option 3.1) is preferred. Final structure to be confirmed by UAE tax counsel before first distribution.

### 3.3 Tax implications (indicative, subject to counsel)

Under UAE Federal Decree-Law 47/2022 (Corporate Tax):

- **Agency (Dubai Mainland LLC).** 9 % CT on taxable income above AED 375,000 (with Small Business Relief likely applicable in Year 1). Service fee to Platform is a deductible expense.
- **Platform (ADGM HoldCo).** If ADGM entity qualifies as Qualifying Free Zone Person (QFZP), income from qualifying activities (IP licensing, platform services to foreign parties) is taxed at 0 %. Income from Mainland UAE sources (i.e., the service fee from the Agency) is generally taxable at 9 % above AED 375,000.
- **VAT** — Inter-company services between related UAE entities are generally subject to 5 % VAT (standard rate); subject to zero-rating if specific criteria met.

Tax counsel to confirm final position and document transfer-pricing methodology. First distribution in Q3 2026 proceeds only after counsel sign-off.

The dividend declaration and payment cycle is:

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Q1 (Jan–Mar) | 31 March | 30 April | 15 May | 31 May |
| Q2 (Apr–Jun) | 30 June | 31 July | 15 August | 31 August |

| Q4 (Oct–Dec) | 31 December | 31 January | 15 February | 28 February |

The first declaration expected is Q3 2026 (payment by 30 November 2026), following the first closed deals in July–September 2026.

### 4.1 Triggers for declaration

A dividend declaration for a quarter is made if all of the following are satisfied:

- Quarter has closed and accounts finalised.
- Distributable Net Profit (per §2.1) is positive.
- Contractual Operating Reserve (§2.1) is maintained or will be maintained after distribution.
- Statutory Reserve requirement (§2.1) is met year-to-date.
- Board resolves to declare (Board majority = 2 of 3, as per `TERM_SHEET.md` §12).

If any trigger fails, the quarter's profit accumulates into retained earnings. In the next quarter, the accumulated amount becomes distributable subject to the same tests.

### 4.2 Bookkeeping and audit

Each quarterly distribution is:

- Recorded in the accounting system (Xero / Zoho / QuickBooks) with a dated Board-resolution reference.
- Paid via the Agency's operating bank account (the same account Rudi has optional real-time read access to, per `TERM_SHEET.md` §15(d)).
- Included in the quarterly management accounts sent to Rudi (within 45 days of quarter end).
- Reviewed annually as part of the ADGM-required audit of the Platform (and any voluntary audit of the Agency).

## 5. Board approval process

### 5.1 Quarterly Board meeting

The Board meets at least once per quarter, within 45 days of quarter end, with the dividend declaration as a standing agenda item.

### 5.2 Quorum and voting

- Quorum: 2 of 3 directors (Zhan, Dymo, Rudi).
- Voting: simple majority.
- In the rare event of a 1-1 vote where one director is absent, the motion is deferred to the next meeting or decided by written unanimous consent.

### 5.3 What Rudi sees

As a Board member, Rudi receives (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §15):

- Board pack at least 5 business days before the meeting, including: quarter's management accounts, proposed Distributable Net Profit calculation, proposed dividend resolution.
- Right to ask questions, request clarifications, and record dissent (although Rudi does not have veto rights).
- Final Board resolution copy within 3 business days of the meeting.

### 5.4 What happens if Rudi dissents

Rudi has no veto. If Rudi disagrees with the calculation or distribution, he can:

- Record his dissent in the Board minutes.
- Request independent accounting or audit review at the Company's expense (within reasonable limits; this right is governed by the Company's Articles of Association).
- Exercise information rights (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §15) to obtain further data.

The dividend is nonetheless declared and paid if 2 of 3 directors (Zhan + Dymo) approve.

## 6. Minimum reserve rule

The Company must maintain a **Contractual Operating Reserve** equal to at least three (3) months of forecast OpEx at all times, before any dividend is declared.

- Year 1: forecast OpEx ~AED 145,000/month. Reserve = AED 435,000 minimum.
- Year 2: forecast OpEx ~AED 260,000/month. Reserve = AED 780,000 minimum.
- Year 3: forecast OpEx ~AED 540,000/month. Reserve = AED 1,620,000 minimum.
- Year 4+: scales with OpEx; reviewed annually at AGM.

This rule protects the Company from distributing in a way that would threaten solvency or force later capital calls. It also protects Rudi (whose investment thesis depends on Company continuity) more than it constrains him.

## 7. Dispute resolution

### 7.1 Disagreements about calculation

If any Shareholder disagrees with a quarterly Distributable Net Profit calculation, the dispute resolution escalation is:

1. **Board discussion** — raised at or before the next Board meeting.
2. **Independent accounting review** — if unresolved, an independent qualified auditor (Big-4 or equivalent, nominated by the majority of the Board) reviews the calculation at the Company's expense.
3. **ADGM arbitration** — if still unresolved, referred to the ADGM Arbitration Centre (per SHA dispute-resolution clause).

### 7.2 Deadlocks

If the Board is deadlocked (e.g., one director absent, 1-1 vote), the following escalation applies:

1. Deferred to the next scheduled Board meeting.
2. If deadlocked at two consecutive meetings, all three Parties are invited to a mediation session with an agreed mediator (cost shared).
3. If mediation fails, the matter is referred to ADGM arbitration.
