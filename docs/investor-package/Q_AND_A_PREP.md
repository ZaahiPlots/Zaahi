## 1. What if Zhan or Dymo leaves?

Both Founders are subject to 4-year reverse vesting with a 1-year cliff. If either leaves before the cliff, all his equity reverts to the company. Between years 1 and 4, only vested shares are retained; the unvested portion returns to treasury. Rudi's 10 % is fully vested from day one and is unaffected by Founder departures.

*Reference: `TERM_SHEET.md` §19 Founder Vesting; `MOU_RUDI.md` §7.*

## 2. What if agency deal flow is slower than projected?

The financial model stress-tests a Conservative scenario with 50 % fewer deals and the combined Stress C scenario (deal volume −50 % + deal size −30 %). In the worst tested case, runway at month 12 remains AED 900,000 without additional capital — i.e., the Agency remains solvent. A bridge of AED 300–500 K or targeted cost cuts extends runway further. The Platform company does not open until the first deal closes, so Platform-side obligations do not begin until revenue is live.

*Reference: `FINANCIAL_MODEL_V1.md` Tab 6a Sensitivity Analysis, Tab 6 Scenarios.*

## 3. Why ADGM for the Platform instead of Mainland?

ADGM operates under English common law — the jurisdiction tier-1 investors, institutional funds, and acquirers expect. Shareholders Agreement enforceability, share-class flexibility (founder Common vs investor Preferred), and arbitration routes are materially stronger than Dubai Mainland. Setup cost is USD 1,500 / year (Tech Startup Licence), cheaper than DIFC. The Agency must be Mainland because RERA broker licences are Dubai Government licences — we get the best of both structures.

*Reference: `INVESTOR_PACKAGE_RESEARCH.md` Part 2.1 (on `research/investor-package` branch).*

## 4. Why a 7-year redemption right?

It is a guaranteed exit floor, not an expected exercise. Most early-stage private investments have no exit path outside M&A or IPO. The Year 7 redemption at Fair Market Value with a 12-month financing window removes that uncertainty. By year 7, ZAAHI's Master Tree is mature enough to support either a Series B / C secondary liquidity event or the buyback from operating cash flow. Rudi is never trapped as a shareholder.

*Reference: `TERM_SHEET.md` §10; `MOU_RUDI.md` §5 (Redemption right).*

## 6. What happens if we disagree on dividend policy?

The Board is three directors (Zhan, Dymo, Rudi). A dividend declaration requires simple majority (2 of 3). If Rudi disagrees with a proposed declaration, he can record his dissent in the minutes, request independent accounting review at Company expense, or exercise information rights for deeper data — but the declaration proceeds if both Founders approve. In practice, dividends are formulaic: 10 / 10 / 10 / 70 of Distributable Net Profit after reserves, calculable by anyone from the financials.

*Reference: `PROFIT_DISTRIBUTION_MECHANICS.md` §5 Board approval process; `TERM_SHEET.md` §12 Governance.*

## 8. What if you accept a higher-valuation Series A and my percentage drops?

Rudi's equity in both the Agency and the Platform is protected by full-ratchet anti-dilution to IPO. On any future equity issuance that would dilute Rudi below 10 % in either entity, additional Preferred Shares are issued to Rudi at no additional cost, restoring his 10 %. The same protection applies to Dymo. Zhan dilutes with Series rounds, floored at 10 %. All dilution from future rounds falls on Zhan and the company treasury pool — not on Rudi.

*Reference: `TERM_SHEET.md` §8 Anti-Dilution; `MOU_RUDI.md` §3.*

## 10. Can I exit before IPO?

Yes, via three independent routes. First, natural exits — M&A or Series rounds — provide pro-rata liquidity on the standard investor timeline. Second, Right of First Refusal transfers from Year 2 let Rudi sell to any third party, subject to ZAAHI and the other shareholders having a 30-day first-match option. Third, Year 7 redemption at Fair Market Value forces a buyback with a 12-month financing window. Rudi is never locked in indefinitely.

*Reference: `TERM_SHEET.md` §10 Redemption, §16 Transfer Restrictions.*

## 11. What if ZAAHI raises from another investor on better terms in the next year?

Most Favoured Nation clause covers exactly this. For 24 months after Rudi's investment, if any future investor receives any economic term more favourable than Rudi's — lower valuation cap, higher liquidation preference multiple, deeper anti-dilution, stronger information rights, better redemption — Rudi automatically inherits the more favourable term at no additional consideration. The Term Sheet commits to this; the Shareholders Agreement codifies it.

*Reference: `TERM_SHEET.md` §9 Most Favoured Nation.*
