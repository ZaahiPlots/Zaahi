## Framing

The terms below are structured to provide Rudi maximum investor comfort while preserving founder operational authority. Anti-dilution, liquidation preference, Most Favoured Nation treatment, and a Year 7 redemption right are set at investor-favourable levels. Governance gives Rudi board visibility without veto power — respecting that operational speed is critical to platform execution. The 1.5× non-participating liquidation preference, full-ratchet anti-dilution to IPO, super pro-rata rights up to 15 %, exclusion from drag-along, and comprehensive information rights (monthly + quarterly + annual + optional real-time bank access + 48-hour material-event notifications) collectively eliminate reasonable reasons for Rudi not to sign.

## 7. Liquidation Preference

**Legal language.**
> In the event of a Liquidity Event or Dissolution Event, the Investor shall be entitled to receive, in priority to the holders of Common Shares, an amount equal to 1.5× the Purchase Amount (i.e., AED 1,500,000), plus any declared but unpaid dividends, being a **non-participating** liquidation preference. **Plain English.**
On a sale, IPO, or wind-up, Rudi gets the better of: (a) 1.5× his AED 1 M investment = AED 1.5 M back first before others, or (b) his 10 % pro-rata share of the proceeds.

**Rationale.**
A 1.5× liquidation preference is above the standard 1× and is calibrated to the maximum-comfort philosophy of this package. It guarantees Rudi recovers 50 % more than his principal in any downside scenario, while the non-participating structure ensures that on an upside exit he simply takes his pro-rata 10 % without dragging founder economics. This clause is unusually favourable for a minority silent-partner investor and reflects the design intent to eliminate any reason for Rudi not to sign.

## 8. Anti-Dilution Protection

**Legal language.**
> The Investor's shareholding in each of the Agency and the Platform shall be protected by **full-ratchet anti-dilution** up to and including an initial public offering. In the event of any issuance of equity, convertible securities, SAFEs, or similar instruments that would dilute the Investor's ownership percentage below 10.0 % of the fully-diluted equity of the relevant entity, the Company shall issue additional Preferred Shares to the Investor at no additional cost, in a quantity sufficient to restore the Investor's ownership to 10.0 %. This protection shall equally apply to the Co-founder (Dymo) on identical terms. The Founder (Zhan) shall dilute in the ordinary course of future financings, subject to a contractual floor of 10.0 % ownership in each entity.

**Plain English.**
Rudi and Dymo both keep their 10 % in perpetuity, whatever future rounds dilute others. Zhan's percentage can fall as rounds come in, but never below 10 %. All the dilution from new rounds falls on Zhan and the company treasury pool.

**Rationale.**
Full-ratchet anti-dilution to IPO is an unusual level of investor protection. In institutional practice, a weighted-average anti-dilution is more common. The founders accept full-ratchet here because (a) Rudi is committing early-stage capital and deserves protection against later dilutive events, (b) the 10 % floor on Zhan preserves long-term founder alignment, and (c) the arithmetic is simple and auditable. Counsel will confirm enforceability of full-ratchet under ADGM and Dubai Mainland law; if a mechanical alternative yields the same economic outcome more enforceably, that may be substituted.

Year 7 is deliberately chosen to align with the natural exit horizon of the Master Tree roadmap (platform maturity, potential Series A–C completion, M&A optionality) while not creating near-term cash-call pressure on the Company. ## 11. Pro-Rata Rights and Super Pro-Rata

**Legal language.**
> On any future issuance of equity or securities convertible into equity, the Investor shall have: (a) a pro-rata right to purchase such portion of the new issuance as is necessary to maintain his then-current ownership percentage (standard pro-rata); and (b) a super pro-rata right to purchase additional securities beyond the pro-rata entitlement, such that the Investor's aggregate ownership does not exceed fifteen per cent (15.0 %) of the fully-diluted equity of either entity after such purchase.

**Plain English.**
When the Company raises money in future rounds, Rudi can buy enough of the new shares to keep his 10 % (that's the "pro-rata" right). He can also buy more than that if he wants — up to a total of 15 % ownership (that's the "super pro-rata" right). Both are rights, not obligations. If he doesn't participate, he just dilutes normally and the anti-dilution protection kicks in to restore his 10 %.

**Rationale.**
Super pro-rata up to 15 % gives Rudi the option to double down on ZAAHI's success. If the Company is compounding value, Rudi has a mechanism to increase exposure. Capping at 15 % preserves founder control. The anti-dilution safety net means if Rudi chooses not to participate, he still keeps his 10 %.

## 17. Tag-Along Rights

**Legal language.**
> In the event of a proposed sale by any Founder of shares representing more than twenty-five per cent (25.0 %) of their collective holdings, the Investor shall have the right (but not the obligation) to participate in such sale on the same terms and on a pro-rata basis.

**Plain English.**
If Zhan or Dymo (or both together) sell more than a quarter of their shares, Rudi can sell the same proportion of his shares on the same terms. This stops the founders from selling out and leaving Rudi stranded.

**Rationale.**
Tag-along is a minority-investor protection against founder partial exits. The 25 % threshold means small transfers (e.g., family-planning-related) do not trigger. The pro-rata mechanism means Rudi's participation is proportional to the founders' sale.

## 18. Drag-Along — Excluded

**Legal language.**
> The Investor shall be expressly excluded from any drag-along obligation. The Company and the Founders acknowledge that, in the event of a proposed sale of the entire Company to a third party, the Investor shall be entitled to elect, at his sole discretion, whether to (a) sell his shares on the same terms alongside the Founders, or (b) retain his shares in the surviving entity (if applicable) or receive cash consideration equivalent to his pro-rata share of the sale proceeds plus any applicable Liquidation Preference.

**Plain English.**
If Zhan and Dymo sell ZAAHI to someone, Rudi is not forced to sell alongside them. He can choose to sell or keep his shares.

**Rationale.**
This is an unusual and deliberate exclusion. In most minority-investor agreements, the majority can "drag" the minority into a sale on the same terms — to enable clean 100 % acquisitions. By excluding Rudi from drag-along, the Company accepts that a future acquirer may need to structure around Rudi's continued shareholding. This is a materially investor-favourable clause and reflects the maximum-comfort design of this package.

## 19. Founder Vesting

**Legal language.**
> Zhan and Dymo shall subject their existing and future shares in each entity to reverse vesting over four (4) years from the Effective Date, with a one (1) year cliff. Upon involuntary termination without Cause (as defined in the SHA), twenty-five per cent (25 %) of then-unvested shares shall accelerate. Upon a change of control combined with termination (the "double-trigger"), 100 % of unvested shares shall accelerate. The Investor's shares shall be fully vested immediately on Closing.

**Plain English.**
Zhan and Dymo each have a "leavers clock": if either quits or gets fired before finishing the 4 years, they lose their unvested shares (which return to the company treasury). The first year is all-or-nothing — if they leave in Year 1, they keep nothing. Rudi has no such clock; his shares are his from day one.

**Rationale.**
Founder vesting is mandatory in every investor-grade venture structure. It protects Rudi against the scenario where a founder walks away and keeps their equity for free. The 4-year / 1-year-cliff is industry standard. Double-trigger acceleration is a protection for the founders against a hostile acquirer that would otherwise terminate them immediately post-close.
