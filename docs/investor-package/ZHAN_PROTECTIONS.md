## Orientation

This document specifies three founder-specific protections for Zhan Ryspayev as Founder and CEO/CTO of ZAAHI:

1. **Performance Bonus Shares** — additional equity of up to 15 % cumulative, triggered by milestone achievement.
2. **Founder Salary Floor** — guaranteed minimum monthly compensation while actively engaged.
3. **Tag-Along Rights and Right of First Refusal** — on any share transfer by Rudi.

These protections are independent of and additional to:

- Zhan's base equity (45 % of Agency; 80 % of Platform) as set out in `MOU_RUDI.md` §2 and `TERM_SHEET.md` §6.
- Zhan's dilution floor of 10 % in each entity (per `MOU_RUDI.md` §3 and `TERM_SHEET.md` §8).
- Zhan's 4-year reverse vesting with 1-year cliff (per `MOU_RUDI.md` §7 and `TERM_SHEET.md` §19).

These protections are codified in a dedicated schedule to the Shareholders Agreement, approved by all three Shareholders (Zhan, Dymo, Rudi). None of them reduce Rudi's economic entitlements or governance rights; they are funded from the Platform Development Fund (ADGM HoldCo) or from the founder equity pool, not from Rudi's 10 %.

## 1. Performance Bonus Shares

### 1.1 Summary

Zhan is entitled to receive additional equity in the Platform (ADGM HoldCo) — called **Performance Bonus Shares** — upon achievement of specified performance milestones. Each milestone triggers **+5.0 % additional equity**, up to a **cumulative maximum of 15.0 %**.

### 1.2 Triggers

Any one of the following, each independently, triggers a single 5 % bonus issuance:

**Trigger A — Platform Annual Recurring Revenue reaches AED 10,000,000.**
Measured as the sum of recurring platform revenue streams over the trailing 12 months (broker SaaS subscriptions, developer SaaS subscriptions, Ambassador tier recurring fees, service fees on platform-routed deals, AI premium access, data licensing). One-off revenue (e.g., single-deal brokerage commission) is excluded. Validation by the Company's annual audit or by a review performed by a Big-4 accounting firm at the Company's election.

**Trigger B — 100 agency deals closed.**
Measured as the cumulative count of deals where DLD transfer certificates have been issued and commissions recorded. Each deal counts once regardless of size. Validation by Board minutes and Agency accounting records.

**Trigger C — Successful completion of a Series A equity financing of not less than USD 2,000,000.**
Measured as: (a) execution of a priced equity financing agreement, (b) receipt of funds, and (c) closing of the round with at least one institutional lead investor. SAFEs, convertible notes, or friends-and-family rounds do not count; a formal priced round with institutional participation is required. Validation by the Board resolution and receipt of funds into the Platform (ADGM HoldCo) bank account.

### 1.3 Maximum cap

Total cumulative Performance Bonus Shares issued to Zhan shall not exceed **15.0 % of the fully-diluted equity of the Platform** (ADGM HoldCo), representing three completed triggers. Once the 15 % cap is reached, no further Performance Bonus Shares are issued regardless of additional milestone achievements.

### 1.4 Vesting and issuance timing

Performance Bonus Shares are:

- **Issued within 30 days** of the Board's verification of the milestone achievement.
- **Fully vested immediately upon issuance** (no additional vesting period, unlike Zhan's base equity).
- Subject to the same transfer restrictions, ROFR, and tag-along provisions as Zhan's base equity.

### 1.5 Source of shares

Performance Bonus Shares are issued as newly-issued shares of the Platform (ADGM HoldCo). To preserve Rudi's and Dymo's anti-dilution protection, the issuance is structured such that:

- Rudi's 10.0 % of the Platform is preserved (full anti-dilution to IPO).
- Dymo's 10.0 % of the Platform is preserved (full anti-dilution to IPO).
- The bonus equity is effectively drawn from Zhan's own base equity pool (diluting Zhan's own share pro-rata) or from the company treasury reserve.

**Mechanism.** At issuance, Zhan's fully-diluted ownership of the Platform moves from his then-current percentage to his then-current percentage plus 5.0 %. Rudi and Dymo keep their 10.0 % each through anti-dilution issuance. The residual is absorbed by (a) any treasury shares, or (b) prior dilution of Zhan's own base. This is a contractual structure that preserves Rudi's and Dymo's economics.

### 1.6 Example scenarios

**Scenario A — Platform reaches AED 10 M ARR in Year 4.**
Trigger A fires. Zhan receives 5 % additional equity. Cumulative bonus issued: 5 %. Maximum remaining: 10 %.

**Scenario B — After 18 months, 100 agency deals are closed (Trigger B) AND a USD 2 M Series A closes (Trigger C).**
Both triggers fire. Zhan receives 10 % additional equity (5 % per trigger). Cumulative bonus issued: 10 %. Maximum remaining: 5 %.

**Scenario C — All three triggers fire over Years 2–5.**
Zhan receives the full 15 % cumulative bonus. Maximum reached. No further bonuses.

**Scenario D — Trigger A fires twice (Platform ARR reaches AED 10 M, then 20 M, then 30 M).**
Each unique trigger fires only once. Trigger A at AED 10 M fires. Subsequent growth does not re-fire Trigger A. The next bonus requires Trigger B or Trigger C.

### 1.7 Founder departure

If Zhan voluntarily departs or is terminated for Cause (as defined in the SHA) before a trigger fires, the opportunity for that particular trigger lapses. If Zhan is terminated without Cause, triggers achieved within the following 12 months are still honoured (same terms, same issuance mechanism).

## 2. Founder Salary Floor

### 2.1 Summary

While Zhan is actively engaged as CEO/CTO of the Platform, the Platform (ADGM HoldCo) shall pay Zhan a **monthly salary of not less than AED 30,000–50,000**, calibrated specifically at SHA execution based on the Company's then-current cash position and the prevailing UAE market rate for founder-CEO compensation at a pre-Series-A technology company.

### 2.2 Calibration

The specific Founder Salary figure within the AED 30,000–50,000 range is calibrated at SHA execution and reviewed annually (at the Annual General Meeting). Suggested calibration framework:

| Year | Suggested base salary (AED / month) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 (May 2026 – Apr 2027) | 35,000 – 40,000 | Company in launch phase; first deals establishing revenue flow |
| Year 2 (May 2027 – Apr 2028) | 40,000 – 45,000 | Revenue validated; team scaling; pre-Series A |
| Year 3 (May 2028 – Apr 2029) | 45,000 – 50,000 | Post-Series A (if raised); platform revenue compounding |
| Year 4+ | Market-benchmarked | Post-platform-maturity; benchmark to UAE tech CEO market |

These are illustrative. The final calibration is agreed at SHA execution.

### 2.3 Payable source

- Salary is paid by the **Platform (ADGM HoldCo)** — not the Agency. This aligns Zhan's compensation with the Platform entity he leads.
- Source of funds: the 70 % Platform Development Fund (per `PROFIT_DISTRIBUTION_MECHANICS.md`), and in Year 1 initially from the launch capital allocated to founder compensation (per `PITCH_DECK_v1.md` slide 16 Use of Funds).
- Tax and social security: treated as employment income under UAE law; no UAE personal income tax; end-of-service gratuity accrued per UAE Labour Law provisions.

### 2.4 Conditions

- Payable **monthly from the Platform (ADGM HoldCo) operating account; pro-rated for partial months**.
- Payable while Zhan is actively engaged as CEO/CTO of the Platform (CTO-only, CEO-only, or combined roles during different phases).
- Suspended if Zhan voluntarily takes extended leave of absence exceeding 30 consecutive days (except for medically-justified leave or Board-approved sabbatical).
- **Terminated** if Zhan voluntarily resigns.
- **Terminated** if Zhan is terminated for Cause (as defined in the Shareholders Agreement).
- **6 months' severance** paid as a lump sum or monthly continuation if Zhan is terminated without Cause.

### 2.5 Priority

The Founder Salary is a priority operating expense of the Platform. It accrues monthly and must be paid before any quarterly dividend declaration. If in any quarter the Platform's cash position cannot support both the salary and a dividend, the salary is paid first and the dividend is deferred or reduced.

### 2.6 Relationship with Dymo compensation

Dymo receives his own salary as Operations Principal of the Agency, payable from the Agency. The Founder Salary Floor is specific to Zhan and does not create a parallel obligation to Dymo — Dymo's compensation is a separate commercial arrangement documented in his employment contract with the Agency. (Dymo may, if he chooses, seek comparable contractual protections, but these are not part of this Addendum.)

## 3. Tag-Along Rights and Right of First Refusal on Rudi transfers

### 3.1 Summary

If Rudi proposes to transfer any of his shares to a third party, Zhan is entitled to:

- **Tag-Along Right** — sell a proportional amount of his own shares on the same terms; and
- **Right of First Refusal (ROFR)** — match the third-party offer and acquire Rudi's shares instead.

These rights are in addition to (and coordinate with) any Company-level ROFR (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §16) and any Dymo-level matching rights.

### 3.2 Tag-Along Right

Tag-Along is triggered whenever Rudi delivers notice of a proposed transfer of any of his shares after the second anniversary of the Closing Date (the earliest date Rudi may transfer per `TERM_SHEET.md` §16(a)).

On receipt of Rudi's transfer notice, Zhan shall have **30 days** to elect to exercise Tag-Along. If exercised:

- Zhan may sell up to a proportional amount of his own shares in the same entity (Agency or Platform) on the same per-share terms as Rudi's proposed sale.
- The proportion is Zhan's-holding / (Rudi-holding + Zhan-holding) applied to the total number of shares being sold.
- The third-party purchaser is obligated to purchase the additional shares on the same economic terms or the transfer cannot proceed.

**Purpose.** Tag-Along prevents Rudi from exiting on terms that Zhan cannot match, leaving Zhan trapped with an unknown new shareholder.

### 3.3 Right of First Refusal

Independently of Tag-Along, Zhan has a ROFR on Rudi's proposed transfer. On receipt of Rudi's transfer notice, Zhan has **30 days** to elect to purchase Rudi's shares on the same economic terms as the third-party offer.

- If Zhan exercises ROFR, he purchases Rudi's shares at the third-party price.
- Zhan's ROFR is subordinate to the Company's ROFR (per `TERM_SHEET.md` §16(a)) — i.e., if the Company elects to purchase, Zhan's ROFR lapses.
- Zhan's ROFR is in parallel with any Dymo ROFR (if Dymo has equivalent rights); if both Zhan and Dymo elect to exercise, the shares are divided pro-rata to their respective shareholdings.

**Purpose.** ROFR gives Zhan direct control over who becomes a shareholder. It is stronger than Tag-Along because Zhan can unilaterally consolidate rather than merely participate.

### 3.4 Sequencing

The sequencing of rights on a Rudi transfer notice is:

1. **Company ROFR** — 30 days. If exercised, the Company purchases Rudi's shares (treasury shares).
2. **Zhan ROFR + Dymo ROFR** (pro-rata) — a further 30 days if Company declines.
3. **Zhan Tag-Along + Dymo Tag-Along** (pro-rata) — a further 30 days if neither ROFR is exercised.
4. **Third-party transfer** — the remaining shares are transferred to the third party on the original proposed terms.

Total maximum timeline from Rudi's notice to final transfer: 90 days.

### 3.5 Permitted transfers

The Tag-Along and ROFR do not apply to Rudi's permitted transfers (as defined in `TERM_SHEET.md` §16(b)): transfers to wholly-owned entities of Rudi, to a family trust or estate structure, or with the prior written consent of the other Shareholders.

### 3.6 Example

Rudi proposes to sell his 10 % of the Agency to a third-party fund for AED 1,500,000.

1. The Company has 30 days to match at AED 1,500,000. It declines (cash preservation).
2. Zhan and Dymo each have 30 days to exercise ROFR. Zhan has 45 % of the Agency, Dymo has 45 %. If both exercise, they purchase Rudi's 10 % equally, 5 % each, at AED 750,000 each. If only Zhan exercises, he purchases the full 10 % at AED 1,500,000.
3. If both decline ROFR, they can exercise Tag-Along: each may sell a proportional amount of their own shares to the third party. Zhan's proportional amount = 45 / (45 + 10) = 81.8 % of his holding. Practically, Zhan and Dymo would elect Tag-Along only if the third-party price is attractive relative to hold economics.
4. If both decline everything, the third-party purchase of Rudi's 10 % proceeds.
