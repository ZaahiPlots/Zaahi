63 Compliance refers to the mandatory legal framework enforced by the Dubai Land Department (DLD) requiring real estate transactions in Dubai to undergo rigorous due diligence, documentation, and verification processes to ensure transparency, legality, and risk mitigation. It applies to all property transactions, including sales, leases, and investments, and is a cornerstone of Dubai’s real estate regulatory environment.  

Key concepts:  
1. Due diligence verification of property ownership, legal status, and title clarity.  
2. Mandatory property documentation, including title deeds, no-objection certificates, and developer approvals.  
3. Anti-money laundering (AML) checks for all parties involved.  
4. Transaction transparency through centralized record-keeping and audit trails.  
5. Legal compliance with UAE Real Estate Law (Law No. 11 of 2013) and Prevention of Money Laundering Law (Law No. 17 of 2013).  
6. Data privacy and protection under UAE Data Protection Law (Law No. 38 of 2023).  
7. Stakeholder verification, including buyers, sellers, and financial institutions.  
8. Tax compliance with Dubai’s real estate tax regulations.  
9. Integration of digital tools for real-time compliance monitoring.  
10. Risk mitigation through automated validation of transactional data.  

UAE regulations:  
- DLD mandates 63 Compliance for all property transactions, ensuring legal validity and preventing fraud.  
- Real Estate Law (Law No. 11 of 2013) governs property ownership, sales, and lease agreements.  
- Prevention of Money Laundering Law (Law No. 17 of 2013) requires AML checks for all parties.  
- Data Protection Law (Law No. 38 of 2023) enforces data security and privacy for user information.  
- DLD’s 2023 updates emphasize digital compliance tools and blockchain integration for transparency.  

Technology applications:
