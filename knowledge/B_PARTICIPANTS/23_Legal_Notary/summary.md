# 23 Legal Notary


23 Legal Notary refers to Law No. 23 of 2004, the UAE Notary Law, which governs notarization processes, legal document verification, and the role of notaries in ensuring the authenticity of documents. Key concepts include: (1) notarization as legal validation of documents, (2) notary’s role in verifying identities and document authenticity, (3) mandatory notarization for real estate transactions, (4) digital notarization under UAE regulations, (5) compliance with the UAE Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA) and Dubai Land Department (DLD) mandates, (6) notary’s liability for errors, (7) electronic notarization systems, (8) integration with blockchain for secure record-keeping, (9) data privacy under UAE Cybercrime Law, (10) cross-border notarization requirements. UAE regulations mandate notarization for property transfers, contracts, and legal agreements, with strict adherence to RERA and DLD guidelines. Technology applications include AI-driven document validation, e-notarization platforms, blockchain for immutable records, and cloud-based compliance tools. Zaahi’s business model leverages these technologies to streamline real estate transactions, ensuring legal compliance while enhancing user trust. Required data points for the platform include property titles, notary seals, digital signatures, transaction timestamps, and compliance certificates.
