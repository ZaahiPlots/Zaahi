38 Dispute Resolution refers to the legal mechanisms and procedures established under UAE law to address conflicts arising from real estate transactions, including property sales, leases, and contractual obligations. It encompasses mediation, arbitration, and court litigation as primary methods for resolving disputes.  

Key concepts:  
1. Mediation: A voluntary, confidential process facilitated by a neutral third party to resolve disputes without court intervention.  
2. Arbitration: A binding resolution method where parties agree to submit disputes to an independent arbitrator, governed by UAE Arbitration Law (Federal Law No. 6 of 2018).  
3. Court Litigation: Adjudication through UAE civil courts, applicable when mediation/arbitration fails, under the Civil Procedure Law (Federal Law No. 5 of 1985).  
4. Contractual Dispute Clauses: Mandatory provisions in real estate agreements specifying resolution mechanisms, timelines, and jurisdiction.  
5. Legal Compliance: Adherence to UAE Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) guidelines and Dubai Multi Commodities Centre (DMCC) regulations.  
6. Evidence Preservation: Requirement to maintain digital and physical records of transactions, contracts, and communications.  
7. Expert Witnesses: Use of specialists in property valuation, construction, or legal matters to substantiate claims.  
8. Settlement Agreements: Binding resolutions reached through negotiation, often formalized in written contracts.  
9. Jurisdictional Clauses: Specification of applicable courts or arbitration institutions (e.g., Dubai International Arbitration Centre).  
10. Time Limits: Statutory deadlines for initiating disputes (e.g., 10 years for property-related claims under UAE law).  

UAE Regulations:  
- Federal Law No. 5 of 1985 (Civil Procedure Law) outlines court procedures and dispute resolution frameworks.  
- Federal Law No. 6 of 2018 (Arbitration Law) mandates enforceability of arbitration awards and procedural standards.  
- RERA regulations (Decree No. 33 of 2013) require real estate platforms to ensure transparency, dispute resolution mechanisms, and consumer protection.  
- DMCC rules for commercial properties emphasize mediation before litigation.
