# 35 Tokenization


Tokenization is the process of converting real estate assets into digital tokens on a blockchain, enabling fractional ownership and liquidity. Zaahi, a Dubai-based real estate platform, leverages tokenization to democratize investment in high-value properties. Key concepts include fractional ownership, smart contracts, token standards (e.g., ERC-20), liquidity, regulatory compliance, token issuance, market access, risk management, and ESG alignment. UAE regulations under the Dubai Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA) and the Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA) mandate transparency, investor protection, and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance. Technology applications include blockchain platforms, smart contracts for automated transactions, token management systems, and data analytics for market trends. Zaahi’s business model benefits from reduced entry barriers, cross-border investment, and enhanced transparency. Required data points for the platform include property details, legal documentation, ownership structure, market data, transaction history, compliance records, investor profiles, risk assessments, and ESG metrics.
