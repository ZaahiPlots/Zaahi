# 56 DAO Treasury


56 DAO Treasury is a decentralized autonomous organization (DAO) framework designed to manage funds, assets, and decision-making processes through programmable rules and community governance. It leverages blockchain technology to enable transparent, secure, and trustless management of real estate investments and other digital assets.  

Key concepts: token-based governance, smart contract execution, liquidity management, decentralized governance, treasury protocols, stakeholder alignment, risk mitigation, scalability, transparency, and integration with real estate assets.  

UAE regulations: The Virtual Currency Regulatory Framework (2023) by the Central Bank of the UAE (CBU) allows limited crypto activities, emphasizing compliance with anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) protocols. The Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA) oversees financial services, requiring platforms to register and adhere to licensing standards. The UAE’s Dubai Blockchain Strategy (2022) promotes blockchain adoption in real estate, enabling tokenization of assets while maintaining legal compliance.  

Technology applications: Smart contracts automate transaction execution and fund distribution; blockchain ensures immutable audit trails for property ownership and transactions; AI-driven analytics optimize investment strategies; IoT devices monitor property conditions; and NFTs tokenize real estate assets for fractional ownership.  

Business model relevance: 56 DAO Treasury aligns with the Zaahi platform’s goal of digitizing Dubai’s real estate market by enabling decentralized investment, community-driven decision-making, and efficient fund management. It supports tokenization of properties, enhancing liquidity and accessibility for global investors while complying with UAE regulatory frameworks.  

Required data points for the platform: Property details (location, size, value, legal status), transaction history, user profiles (KYC/AML compliance), governance metrics (vote counts, proposal approvals), compliance documentation, market analytics (demand, pricing trends), smart contract audit reports, NFT metadata (ownership, transfer history), and regulatory compliance records.
