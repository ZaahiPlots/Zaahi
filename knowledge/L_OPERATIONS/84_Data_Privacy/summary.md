# 84 Data Privacy


84 Data Privacy refers to the legal and technical framework governing the collection, storage, processing, and sharing of personal data within the UAE, particularly under the UAE Data Protection Law (Federal Law No. 1 of 2023). It emphasizes the rights of data subjects, organizational accountability, and compliance with strict data governance standards. Key concepts include:  
1. **Data Privacy** – Protection of personal data from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.  
2. **Data Protection Law (DPL)** – UAE’s comprehensive legal framework (Federal Law No. 1 of 2023) governing data handling.  
3. **Consent** – Explicit, informed, and revocable agreement from data subjects for data processing.  
4. **Data Minimization** – Collection and processing of only necessary data for specific purposes.  
5. **Data Security** – Implementation of technical and organizational measures to safeguard data.  
6. **Data Breach Notification** – Mandatory reporting of breaches within 72 hours to the UAE Data Protection Authority (DPA).  
7. **Data Subject Rights** – Rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of personal data.  
8. **Data Localization** – Storage of personal data within UAE borders unless exceptions apply.  
9. **Accountability** – Organizations must demonstrate compliance through documented policies and audits.  
10. **Data Portability** – Ability of data subjects to transfer their data between service providers.  

**UAE Regulations**:  
- **Federal Law No. 1 of 2023** establishes data protection principles, penalties for non-compliance (up to 10% of annual turnover), and the DPA’s oversight role.  
- **Article 84** (if applicable) may outline specific obligations for data controllers and processors, such as breach reporting and third-party compliance.  
- **Penalties**: Fines, operational restrictions, or criminal liability for violations.  

**Technology Applications**:  
- **Encryption**: Protecting data at rest and in transit.  
- **Anonymization/Pseudonymization**: Reducing identifiable data exposure.  
- **Secure APIs**: Ensuring third-party data exchange compliance.  
- **Blockchain**: Immutable records for audit trails and data integrity.  
- **Compliance Tools**: Automated consent management and audit trails.  

**Business Model Relevance**:
