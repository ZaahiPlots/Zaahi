**Definition**: A data centre is a facility housing servers, storage systems, and networking equipment to manage, store, and process data. In the context of Zaahi platform and Dubai UAE real estate, data centres are critical infrastructure for digital transformation, supporting cloud computing, AI, IoT, and smart city initiatives.

1. **Scalability**: Data centres must support growing data demands through modular expansion.  
2. **Security**: Physical and cybersecurity measures (e.g., biometrics, encryption) are non-negotiable.  
3. **Energy Efficiency**: Cooling systems and renewable energy integration are mandated under UAE regulations.  
4. **Colocation**: Third-party businesses rent space in shared data centres for cost-effective infrastructure.  
5. **Hybrid Cloud**: Integration with on-premises and cloud environments to optimize performance.  
6. **Edge Computing**: Low-latency data processing near end-users, critical for real-time applications.  
7. **Data Localization**: UAE laws require data to be stored locally, necessitating onshore data centres.  
8. **Sustainability**: Compliance with Dubai’s Green Building Regulations and Net Zero by 2050 targets.
